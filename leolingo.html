Problem

Speech therapy is inaccessible

Expensive sessions, long waiting lists, and boring at-home exercises. Children disengage — and families are left without tools to support progress between appointments.

Solution

Play as therapy

LeoLingo turns speech exercises into games led by Mojo — a 3D animated lion who makes every session feel like an adventure, not a clinical exercise.

Core Features

What it does

  • Speech-based games and structured exercises
  • Real-time voice interaction with feedback
  • Child-friendly UI — large targets, icon-first nav
  • Mojo — a 3D interactive lion guiding every session

Design Approach

Make the right action the fun action

Every therapeutic action is the natural next step in the game. Children follow Mojo — not instructions. Warm jungle aesthetics. Minimal text. Pure play.

Learning flows and activity screens

"Too gamified and the therapeutic value gets lost. Too clinical and children disengage in minutes. The answer: structured freedom — clear goals, hidden inside play."

Learnings

What I took away

01

Designing for children requires emotional resonance — logic alone doesn't hold attention.

02

How something feels matters as much as what it teaches.

03

A character like Mojo creates continuity — users return for the relationship, not just the feature.
